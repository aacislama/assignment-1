Cherry Blossom

Festival of Japan

A cherry blossom festival, or hanami, is a centuries-old Japanese tradition celebrating the fleeting beauty of cherry blossoms (sakura) and the arrival of spring. While originating in Japan with the hanami picnic culture, these festivals are now celebrated globally, including in the United States and South Korea. Festivals feature picnics, parties, music, and special foods, with timing and location varying annually to coincide with the blossom forecast and celebrate the transient beauty of life.

What happens in this Festival ?

People gather to admire cherry blossoms, which range in color from pink to white.

Friends and families hold outdoor gatherings with food, drink, and music.

Some festivals incorporate dishes made with cherry blossoms.
